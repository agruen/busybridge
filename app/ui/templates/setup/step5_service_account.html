Why use a service account?

When BusyBridge syncs events from client calendars to your main calendar, a service account creates those events as a separate identity. This makes them non-draggable in Google Calendar, preventing accidental moves. Without a service account, synced events can be accidentally repositioned by drag-and-drop.

Your Google Workspace organization has an organization policy (iam.disableServiceAccountKeyCreation) that blocks key creation. An administrator needs to temporarily disable it:

  1. 1. In Google Cloud Console, go to IAM & Admin → Organization Policies.
  2. 2. Search for iam.disableServiceAccountKeyCreation.
  3. 3. Click on Disable service account key creation.
  4. 4. Click Manage Policy (or Edit).
  5. 5. Under Policy source, select Override parent's policy.
  6. 6. Click Add a rule, set Enforcement to Off.
  7. 7. Click Set Policy and try creating the key again.

You only need to create the key once. After downloading it, you can re-enable the policy by setting Enforcement back to On.

Requires the Organization Policy Administrator role (roles/orgpolicy.policyAdmin).
