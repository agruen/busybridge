Welcome to BusyBridge

Let's get your calendar synchronization service set up.

What This App Does

BusyBridge keeps your main calendar synchronized with multiple client organization calendars:

  • Events from client calendars appear on your main calendar with full details
  • Your main calendar events appear as "Busy" blocks on client calendars
  • Changes sync bidirectionally and automatically via webhooks and periodic polling

Before You Begin

This setup wizard will walk you through each step in detail, but here's an overview of what you'll need to do:

You'll create a project in the Google Cloud Console, enable the Google Calendar API, configure an OAuth consent screen, and create OAuth 2.0 credentials (Client ID and Secret). Step 2 of this wizard covers this in detail.
You'll sign in with your Google Workspace account. This becomes the first admin user, and the domain from your email sets who else can use the service.
Optionally configure email alerts for sync failures, then save the encryption key that protects your OAuth tokens at rest.

Requirements

  • A Google account with access to Google Cloud Console
  • This server must be reachable at a public HTTPS URL (for OAuth callbacks and webhooks)
  • About 10 minutes for the full setup
